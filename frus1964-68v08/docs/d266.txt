266. Letter From Secretary of the Treasury Dillon to Secretary of Commerce Hodges
Date: 1964-12-04
Source: Washington National Records Center, RG 40, Secretary of Commerce Files: FRC 69 A 6828, Treasury Department.

Dear Luther:

Your letter of December 2, 1964, asks where we stand on the Canadian auto parts question.

In a meeting held in George Ball's office on Monday, November 23, which you were unable to attend, there was full agreement that countervailing duties should be imposed in early January if the problem arising from the Canadian rebate plan has not been solved before that time. Before taking such action, Treasury would, of course, advise the White House and the Departments of State and Commerce.

With best wishes,

Sincerely,
