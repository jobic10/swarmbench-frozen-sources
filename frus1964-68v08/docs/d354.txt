354. Memorandum for the Record
Date: 1967-05-13
SUBJECT: Kennedy Round Situation Report, May 13, 1967
Source: Johnson Library, Bator Papers, Kennedy Round, May-June 1967, Box 12.

I have had an emergency call for help from Bill Roth via the British. Apparently they are stonewalling a number of critical issues. Bill is worried that this might start a general unravelling with the risk that the entire house of cards will crumble.

We have gone to London on every channel and hit them with all we have, short of personal Presidential intervention with Harold Wilson.

Dean Rusk called George Brown on the telephone. They missed each other, but got George's private secretary before leaving for Hot Springs. Later Gene Rostow talked to Brown and explained the problem.

-David Bruce is going in to see the Prime Minister right now. -Both Gene and I have talked to Michael Stewart here (Pat Dean is unavailable). -I called Harold Wilson's private secretary, Michael Palaser on the direct line. Michael is an old friend and I laid it hard on the line that this is political business of the highest sort, and should receive immediate attention from his boss. I said that the President has been personally involved in managing our Kennedy Round over the past few days, and considers the successful outcome of the negotiations a matter of greatest concern.
