HILDYARD, JOHN (_brother of the preceding_). _b._ 1796 or 1797; ed. at Shrewsbury, head boy there; went to St. John’s coll. Cam., B.A. 1818, M.A. 1821; barrister L.I. 10 July 1821; recorder of Stamford, Grantham and Leicester 1835–54; judge of county courts, circuit 20 (Leicestershire), March 1847 to death. _d._ the King’s hotel, Loughborough 13 Feb. 1855. _bur._ in Townshend vault, All Saints’ church, Hertford 21 Feb. NOTE.—His father lived to see 9 sons masters of arts in Cambridge university, and 6 of them fellows of their respective colleges.
