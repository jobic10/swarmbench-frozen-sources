HARTLEY, JESSE (_son of a bridge master in N.R. Yorkshire_). _b._ near Pontefract 1780; apprentice to a mason; surveyor of the Liverpool docks 1824 to death, constructed or altered every dock there 1824–60; completed the Grosvenor bridge over the river Dee at Chester, which had the largest single span stone arch (200 feet) in existence at the time 1832. _d._ Bootle Marsh near Liverpool 24 Aug. 1860. _Min. of proc. of Instit. of C.E. xxxiii_, 219–22 (1872). NOTE.—His son John Bernard Hartley who was _b._ 3 Sep. 1814 and _d._ 14 Dec. 1869, was joint surveyor of Liverpool docks with his father from July 1847.
