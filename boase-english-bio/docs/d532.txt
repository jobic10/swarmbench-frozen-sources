FINNIS, THOMAS QUESTED (_son of Robert Finnis of Hythe, Kent_). _b._ Hythe, Jany. 1801; partner in firm of Finnis and Fisher 79 Great Tower st. London, provision merchants; the first pioneer of commerce to port of Bussorah; alderman of Lower Ward 18 Jany. 1848; sheriff of London 1848–49, lord mayor 1856–57. _d._ Park Gate, Wanstead, Essex 29 Nov. 1883. _J. E. Ritchie’s Famous city men_ (1884) 96–105; _Illust. news of the world ii_, 333 (1858), _portrait_; _I.L.N. xxix_, 479 (1856), _portrait_, _lxxxiii_, 581 (1883), _portrait_. NOTE.—His brother John Finnis, lieut. col. 11 Bengal N.I. was the first English officer killed in the Sepoy mutiny, at Meerut 10 May 1857 in 54 year, memorial tablet in church of St. Dunstan in the East, London.
